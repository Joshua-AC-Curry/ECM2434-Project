Q: What is this?

This is a tree game. An app developed to promote sustainability on campus at the University of Exeter.


Q: How does the game work?

Bark Buddies is an app developed to promote sustainability on campus at the University of Exeter. You own a pet tree, and the tree releases oxygen. Your goal is to care for the tree so it releases as much oxygen as possible. To do so, you have to water the tree, but if you overwater or underwater it, it will produce oxygen at a less optimal rate. If you don't water the tree enough, it'll die, meaning you'll lose all your oxygen, and the water meter will reset.


Q: How does this app promote sustainability?

You water a tree by using a water fountain on campus. Once you're within a certain distance of a water fountain and click the "Get Current Location" button, you'll water the tree. This game is built on trust, so we'll trust that you'll only water your tree each time you refill your water bottle. The idea is that by promoting the water fountains on campus, instead of buying plastic bottles of water, users will reuse water bottles, saving potential plastic waste.
